# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Interactions
- Collaborate with QA/Testing on acceptance criteria and test coverage
- Work with UX Designer on interface specifications and usability
- Coordinate with Security Lead on secure coding practices
- Partner with Release Manager on deployment readiness

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Interactions
- Work with UX Designer on user research and design validation
- Partner with Data Analyst for metrics, dashboards, and success measurement
- Align with Support Engineer on feature support readiness
- Coordinate with Release Manager on release planning and go/no-go decisions

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Interactions
- Escalate risks and dependencies flagged by Security Lead
- Coordinate release schedules with Release Manager
- Incorporate Data Analyst insights into status reporting
- Facilitate communication between Support Engineer and development team

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers own the user experience, ensuring features are intuitive, accessible, and align with user needs. They collaborate with Product and Development to validate designs and iterate based on user feedback.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Validate design decisions with users and stakeholders
- Collaborate with developers on implementation and edge cases
- Document design patterns and accessibility standards
- Iterate on designs based on feedback and analytics

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce friction and support costs through better design
- Build user confidence and satisfaction

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with product and engineering
- Usability testing reports and findings
- Design system documentation and component specs
- Accessibility and WCAG compliance guidance

### Interactions
- Partner with Product Manager on feature prioritization and user research
- Work with Developers on interface specifications and implementation feasibility
- Collaborate with Data Analyst on usage metrics and UX effectiveness
- Coordinate with Support Engineer on user pain points and feedback loops

---

## Security Lead

### Role Summary
Security Leads identify and mitigate security risks throughout the project lifecycle. They establish secure development practices, review critical changes, and coordinate incident response.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct security reviews and threat modeling
- Establish secure coding standards and practices
- Review architecture, design, and high-risk code changes
- Manage security scanning and vulnerability remediation
- Lead security incident response and post-incident reviews
- Train and coach team members on security practices

### Goals
- Reduce security vulnerabilities and breach risk
- Build security awareness and accountability into development culture
- Enable secure delivery without blocking velocity

### Typical Communication
- Security review findings and recommendations
- Risk registers and mitigation plans
- Security incident alerts and response coordination
- Training and best-practice guidance

### Interactions
- Work with Developers on secure coding and architecture reviews
- Partner with Release Manager on pre-release security validation
- Coordinate with Project Manager on security risk escalation
- Advise Product Manager on security trade-offs and feature impact

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts define metrics, build dashboards, and analyze project and product data to inform decision-making. They provide evidence-based insights to validate outcomes and guide iterations.

### Responsibilities
- Define success metrics aligned with business and product goals
- Build dashboards and monitoring for key signals
- Analyze product usage, performance, and user behavior
- Provide insights and recommendations to product and delivery leadership
- Support post-release validation and continuous improvement
- Document data collection and reporting methodologies

### Goals
- Enable data-driven decision-making across the product and project
- Measure and communicate impact of delivered features
- Identify optimization opportunities and bottlenecks

### Typical Communication
- Weekly or milestone-based dashboards and reports
- Analysis findings and recommendations
- Success metric documentation and baselines
- Post-release impact summaries

### Interactions
- Collaborate with Product Manager on metric definition and success measurement
- Partner with UX Designer on user behavior and engagement analytics
- Work with Release Manager on post-deployment verification and rollout analysis
- Provide insights to Project Manager for status reporting and risk assessment

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers own the release lifecycle, coordinating scheduling, approvals, deployments, and verification. They ensure releases are well-planned, executed safely, and communicated clearly.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and schedule release windows and timelines
- Coordinate release approvals and go/no-go decisions
- Manage release checklists and pre-release validation
- Orchestrate deployments and post-deployment verification
- Communicate release status and timing to stakeholders
- Manage rollback plans and incident response for failed deployments
- Document release notes and migration guidance

### Goals
- Execute predictable, low-risk releases
- Minimize deployment-related incidents and downtime
- Maintain clear communication with stakeholders and support teams

### Typical Communication
- Release planning and scheduling notifications
- Pre-release checklists and approval requests
- Release notes and migration guides
- Go/no-go decision summaries and post-release reports

### Interactions
- Coordinate with Developers on release readiness and artifact delivery
- Work with QA/Testing on pre-release validation and smoke tests
- Partner with Security Lead on security sign-off
- Align with Product Manager and Project Manager on release prioritization
- Notify Support Engineer of release details and known issues

---

## Support Engineer

### Role Summary
Support Engineers ensure the product is ready for users, participate in post-release triage, and channel user feedback back to the product team. They bridge the gap between customers and development.

### Responsibilities
- Validate feature readiness and support documentation
- Triage and investigate user issues and escalations
- Document known issues and workarounds
- Communicate user feedback and pain points to product and development teams
- Participate in incident response and post-incident analysis
- Help train support and customer success teams on new features

### Goals
- Reduce support burden and customer friction
- Improve time-to-resolution for user issues
- Close the feedback loop between customers and product teams

### Typical Communication
- Feature readiness checklists and documentation reviews
- Issue triage reports and escalations
- User feedback summaries and trend analysis
- Post-release and incident retrospectives

### Interactions
- Collaborate with Product Manager on user needs and feature feedback
- Work with Developers on bug investigation and resolution
- Partner with Release Manager on release communication and known issues
- Provide input to UX Designer on user pain points and usability issues
- Support Project Manager with escalation and issue tracking
